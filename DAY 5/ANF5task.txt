### üß© **Example 1**

**Original:**

```scheme
(+ (* 2 3) (+ 4 5))
```

**Step 1:**
Both `(* 2 3)` and `(+ 4 5)` are complex, so let-bind them.

**A-Normal Form:**

```scheme
(let ([t0 (* 2 3)])
  (let ([t1 (+ 4 5)])
    (+ t0 t1)))
```

---

### üß© **Example 2**

**Original:**

```scheme
(if (> (+ x 1) 10) (* y 2) (/ z 3))
```

**Step 1:**
`(+ x 1)` and `(> (+ x 1) 10)` are complex ‚Üí must be let-bound.

**A-Normal Form:**

```scheme
(let ([t0 (+ x 1)])
  (let ([t1 (> t0 10)])
    (if t1
        (let ([t2 (* y 2)]) t2)
        (let ([t3 (/ z 3)]) t3))))
```

---

### üß© **Example 3**

**Original:**

```scheme
(f (g x) (h (+ y 1)))
```

**Step 1:**
`(g x)` and `(h (+ y 1))` are complex, also `(+ y 1)` is complex.

**A-Normal Form:**

```scheme
(let ([t0 (g x)])
  (let ([t1 (+ y 1)])
    (let ([t2 (h t1)])
      (f t0 t2))))
```
